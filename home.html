About The Museum

The Museum is located in a World War I bunker in the James Hall at The American College. The Honorable Baron Carmicheal the then Governor of the Madras Presidency, laid the foundation stone of the James Hall on 1st March 1912. Named after a lady named Ellen S. James, this building is unique. It is noteworthy that Dr. K.S. Krishnan, the first Bhatnagar awardee, was our student at that time. The bunker was designed in accordance with the architectural rules of the British government that buildings should be built with a bunker for security in the event of war. The bunker is roughly 10ft high and extends completely underground with one entrance and four exits. It is built with iron girders manufactured by the famous Borman Enterprises in Newcastle, England and were shipped to The American College. The Museum was inaugurated by Ms. Kanimozhi Karunanidhi, Member of Parliament, Lok Sabha, New Delhi on 19 April 2023. Among the 500+ exhibits in The Museum, neolithic stone axe, 2500 years old black and red wares, ancient coins, palm-leaf manuscripts, ancient weapons, World War arms, Sir C.V. Raman's UV visible spectrometer, Lord Curzon's letter to the college, fossils, herbariums, embalmed specimens and mathematics models are sure to draw attention.
